Semantic Segmentation

Deep Learning

I implement and use AI algorithms to detect a wide variety of defects (>50) on lumber boards like knots, shakes, stains, wormholes, etc. For this, I use Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) . The CNN first transforms the input image into a pixel-wise prediction image thanks to a semantic segmentation. The segmented regions are then classified into specific classes. Along with this, I also develop solutions for evaluating CNN models and then visualize the metrics generated from this evaluation.

The study of wood using machine vision is a challenge for many reasons, but all these reasons start with the fact that wood is a natural and biological material. Wood is not a homogeneous material. Each board is different. Between and within species, there are many different characteristics and rules to follow for grading. In addition to the inherent variability of wood, there is also the coarseness of the rough lumber. Indeed, trees can be subjected to many different climatic conditions that lead to a wide range of defects. For example, with climate change, worm attack trees more and more and sawmills need to have precise detection of where the worm hole are and how often they damage the wood.
